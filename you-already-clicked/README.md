# You already clicked

An interactive e-learning activity for **ABC Training & Consulting — Course 1.9,
Lesson 2: Phishing & Social Engineering** (prepared for ALS).

## What it teaches

Most phishing training stops at "don't click." This one starts *after* the click:
the recovery drill for the five minutes once you realise you've handed over your
password. It reframes fast reporting as the fix, not an admission of failure — and
separates the actions that matter *right now* from the ones that can wait, and from
the tempting mistakes (hiding the email, waiting it out, re-clicking to "check").

## Mechanic

**Sort** — 9 cards, about 5 minutes. Each card is an action. The learner assigns it
to one of three buckets:

- **Next five minutes** (green)
- **Today** (navy)
- **Don't do this** (red)

Nothing is marked until all nine cards have a bucket and **Check my sorting** is
pressed. On check, each card turns green (correct) or red (wrong), shows a verdict
and a short explanation, and a score of *N of 9 sorted correctly* appears with the
closing line. **Start again** replays the activity.

The sort is a button-assignment interaction (not drag-only), so it is fully keyboard
operable and screen-reader friendly: every verdict carries a text label and icon,
never colour alone.

## How to open

Just open `index.html` in any modern browser (double-click it). No build step, no
dependencies, no network connection required — everything is inline in the single file.
